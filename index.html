📚 Course Material Summary

Test Plan vs Test Strategy

Plan = project-specific, dynamic. Strategy = org-wide, static. Plan answers what/who/when. Strategy answers how/why at a higher level.

Test Plan Components

Objectives (WHY), Scope (WHAT), Approach (HOW), Roles/RACI (WHO), Risk Analysis (WHAT could go wrong), Schedule (WHEN), Environments (WHERE), Deliverables (WHAT is produced).

Risk-Based Testing (RBT)

4 steps: Identify risks → Analyze (Likelihood × Impact, 1-5 scale) → Plan & Execute (high-risk first) → Test Closure (residual risk + sign-off). Score bands: 1-9 Low, 10-16 Medium, 17-25 High.

RACI Matrix

R=Responsible (does work), A=Accountable (owns outcome, exactly ONE per task), C=Consulted (provides input), I=Informed (kept updated). Every task needs at least one R and exactly one A.

Test Estimation (WBS)

Break testing into phases: Planning → Design → Setup → Execution → Defect Mgmt → Closure. Estimate per subtask in person-hours. Add 10-25% buffer for uncertainty.

Case Studies

Knight Capital ($440M loss in 45 min — no regression testing), Boeing 737 MAX (346 deaths — no failure simulation), Sony PSN (77M accounts — no penetration testing).

📝 Homework Assignment

Test Like a Risk Manager

Scenario: Online Ticket Booking System — Platform for concerts & events. Features: user login/registration, search & filtering, seat selection & pricing, online payments (card, PayPal, Apple Pay), mobile & desktop, email confirmation with QR code. Team: 3 QA Engineers. Mobile testing via BrowserStack.

Required Tasks:

  1. Identify 7–10 potential risks in the system
  2. Rate each risk by Likelihood and Impact (use the scoring formula: Risk Score = Likelihood × Impact, scale 1-5 each)
  3. Select the top 4 highest-risk areas to test first
  4. For each top risk: why is it high risk | what testing type applies | manual or automated | when to test

Scoring bands from the material: 1–9 = Low (monitor), 10–16 = Medium (plan mitigation), 17–25 = High (test first, prioritize)
